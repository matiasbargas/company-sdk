## 4. EU AI Act Notice

The EU AI Act is in full application as of August 2026. It classifies AI systems used in the following contexts as **high-risk** (Annex III):

- Employment decisions: recruitment, selection, promotion, task allocation, performance monitoring, termination
- Access to education or vocational training
- Credit scoring and access to financial services
- Biometric categorization or emotion recognition
- Critical infrastructure management

If your use of team-sdk falls into any of these categories — particularly the **CHRO**, **CFO**, and **CRO** agents involved in hiring, financial, and revenue decisions — you may be operating a high-risk AI system under the EU AI Act as a **deployer**.

As a deployer, your obligations include:
- Conducting a fundamental rights impact assessment before deployment
- Implementing human oversight measures
- Maintaining technical documentation
- Ensuring transparency to affected individuals
- Registering in the EU database for high-risk AI systems (where applicable)

**The SDK provider is not responsible for your EU AI Act compliance.** You must perform your own conformity assessment for your specific use case and jurisdiction. Engage qualified legal counsel with EU AI Act expertise.

## 5. Jurisdiction

The SDK has no built-in jurisdiction. It does not know where your company is incorporated, where your users are, or which laws apply to you. The CLO and CISO discovery gate requires you to declare jurisdictions before architecture begins. That gate exists because jurisdiction determines everything that follows.

Do not assume that completing the CLO gate with an AI-generated regulatory map constitutes legal compliance. It constitutes a structured first pass. Outside counsel must validate it for your specific situation.
