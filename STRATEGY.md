## The 4 Success Themes

Every agent reasons from these themes. When a decision is ambiguous, ask: which theme does this serve?

### Theme 1 — Faster Delivery of the Right Things
We ship small changes frequently. We focus people on architecture and higher-value decisions, not boilerplate and repetitive tasks. AI and agents take over low-leverage work so we can move faster on the right bets.

**What this looks like:** PMs and teams shipping small changes frequently, not only large releases. Teams focused on architecture and higher-value design. The number of AI agents and skills grows over time as part of normal delivery — not as side projects.

**Metrics that move:** Throughput increases (epics burned, items completed). Cycle time / lead time decreases from idea to production.

### Theme 2 — Higher Quality and Better Customer Experience
We use AI to raise the bar on quality and experience. Fewer bugs, more stable flows, changes that customers actually value.

**What this looks like:** Teams using AI heavily for testing (end-to-end tests, load tests, regression support). AI assisting in reviews and validation of customer-facing flows before launch.

**Metrics that move:** Bugs and production issues reduce. NPS increases. Voice of Customer confirms qualitative improvements.

### Theme 3 — Strong Compliance, Security and Robustness
Compliance, security, and robustness are built into architecture and workflows — not bolted on. AI helps us be more auditable while reducing the manual burden of reporting and audits.

**What this looks like:** Architectures that are self-explainable and auditable. AI supporting creation and maintenance of compliance outputs instead of ad-hoc manual work.

**Metrics that move:** Audit time cut roughly in half. Reporting time cut roughly in half. Less team burden during audit season and month-close.

### Theme 4 — Financial and Operational Efficiency
AI and automation help us run current operations more efficiently, free up capacity, and unlock new revenue. Core processes move toward "autodriver."

**What this looks like:** More automations added across the value chain, especially for recurring structured work. Key processes (transfers, internal reporting, settlements) running on automation.

**Metrics that move:** P&L with more revenue streams. Healthy operations vs. budget. Less time maintaining current operations — more time for new initiatives.

## The Operating Principles

These are not rules. They are decisions you can use to make real calls when you are not sure what to do.

1. **If your team can't use this strategy to make real decisions, it's not a strategy.** Surface the gap.
2. **Strategy is made of principles, not plans.** You can't script the future — but you can guide decisions.
3. **Customers are special.** They bring in the value. Every other stakeholder takes value out. Serve the customer first.
4. **Solve big problems.** The bigger the problem, the more value you create.
5. **Satisfaction over profit.** Happy customers bring profits. Not the other way around.
6. **It all connects.** Customers → Needs → Value → Assets → Processes.
7. **Forget the vision board.** Focus on purpose and mission — who we serve, and why.
8. **Strategy is direction + pace.** Not a fixed destination.
9. **Forget annual targets.** Build the capacity to learn, adapt, and create value.
10. **The better the principles, the less you need rules.** Autonomy with direction is magic.

## The Mission Pod Strategy

We organize by outcomes, not by domain ownership. This is not a statement about hierarchy — it is a statement about focus.

**From:** Fixed squads that "own" a feature set indefinitely → slow prioritization, "not my domain" friction.
**To:** Purpose-built, temporary Mission Pods that form around a high-value "Mission," execute with AI-assisted speed, and dissolve back into the talent pool once the objective is met.

This makes us **outcome-driven rather than component-driven** — deploying our best people to the most urgent business needs in real-time.

Guardians (Lead/Staff Engineers and PMs) remain fixed in domains to ensure that while the mission is temporary, the domain knowledge, architectural standards, and code quality are permanent.

## How Strategy Enters the System

1. **CPTO/Owner** announces strategic direction in the main conversation (the Owner channel).
2. **CEO** frames it, synthesizes it, and routes it through the Bus to all relevant agents.
3. **Coordinator** tracks it as a release-level decision in `history.md` and `strategy-log.md`.
4. **Every agent** writes to their area log when they announce a goal, change a direction, or discover a requirement that intersects with strategy.

Strategy is not a secret. It should be visible in every decision, every mission shaped, every product shipped.

## The Betting Table

How missions get selected. The CPTO/Head of Product runs this.

- Runs at the start of each mission cycle.
- All Appetized missions are reviewed.
- Leadership selects which missions run in the next cycle.
- A mission not selected waits — it does not auto-extend.
- The Betting Table does not estimate. It selects.

The Betting Table output is logged to `strategy-log.md` and `history.md`.
